About Me

My name is Emily Chang, and I'm a second-year student at the University of Washington studying computer science. I’m passionate about creating useful, accessible products that help others in their daily lives whether it’s decision-making or streamlining a process. Since beginning my journey, I’ve created some projects to advance my skills and apply what I know to real-life situations. My projects range from mobile applications that display information to interactive webpages that encourage social distancing.

I am also passionate about STEM education. Growing up, I didn't have much support as I ventured into the world of technology, and I'm very glad that resources are now more widely available for aspiring computer scientists. In high school, I attended the Girls Who Code club to learn some programming basics. Since then, I've worked in computer science outreach and education with organizations like the Society of Women Engineers "SWE++" and STEM League.
